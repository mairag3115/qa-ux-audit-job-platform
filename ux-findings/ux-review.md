\# UX Findings



\## Finding 01 – Search experience may be unclear

The search bar may not guide users clearly about what type of keywords they should enter, such as job title, company, or skill.



\## Finding 02 – Filters may create friction

When multiple filters are applied, it may not be obvious which filters are active or how to remove them easily.



\## Finding 03 – Job information hierarchy

Some job offers may present information in a way that makes it difficult to quickly identify salary, location, contract type, or requirements.



\## Finding 04 – Application flow confidence

Users may need more reassurance during the application process, such as confirmation messages or clearer progress feedback.



\## Finding 05 – Visual overload

If too much information is shown at once, users may feel overwhelmed and have difficulty focusing on the most important details.

\## Finding 06 – Empty results page does not provide direct recovery actions

When the platform returns no job results, the page gives general recommendations such as checking spelling, using synonyms, or adjusting filters. However, it does not provide a direct and visible action to clear filters or quickly modify the current search. This may create friction and slow down the user's recovery process.
